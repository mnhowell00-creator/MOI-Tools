Action Matrix

Every neighborhood mapped across two axes: intervention urgency (MOI score tier) and primary constraint type. The cell a neighborhood lands in determines the type of response most likely to close its gap.

How to read this matrix

The vertical axis shows intervention urgency — Tier 1 (most urgent) at top, Tier 3 (stable) at bottom. The horizontal axis shows the primary constraint driving the low score: Supply (lack of facilities), Delivery (facilities exist but programs don't run), or Coverage (programs run but residents don't participate, or disease burden dominates). The intersection tells you what kind of investment is needed.

Constraint Type Definitions
Supply Constraint: Insufficient facilities, parks, or open space. The primary barrier is physical infrastructure.
Delivery Constraint: Facilities exist but structured programs are not running or are severely underutilized.
Coverage Constraint: Programs and facilities are present but participation rates are low — often linked to cultural, financial, or awareness barriers.
Burden Constraint: Chronic disease prevalence is so high it suppresses movement capacity. Requires health-first intervention before program investment can be effective.
